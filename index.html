James Douglas Fulp III

Jimmy left us in 1992, long before it was common practice for artists to publish their work online for the world to see. As someone who creates websites for a living, I have often thought about what kind of website my Uncle would have if he were alive today. I think it's possible he would have paved the web design path ahead of me, as he was tinkering with digital art before most people had a personal computer. Of course we'll never know, but I have decided to use what I do know to document what he created while he was still here. Please enjoy this collection of Jimmy's art, both fine and commerical. There is also a gallery of personal photos at the end.
